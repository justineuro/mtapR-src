1. What is the place value of the underlined digit in 583, 235?
Ans.: ten thousands Sol.: The given number is equal to 5(100,000) + 8(10,000) + 3(1,000) + 2(100) + 3(10) +5(1). Hence, the place value of the underlined 8 is ten thousands or that the underlined 8 occupies the ten thousands place.


2. Write two hundred eighty five thousand three in figures.
Ans.: 285, 003 Sol.: The given number is equal to 285(1,000) + 3(1) = 285,000 + 3 = 285,003. Hence, the place value of the underlined 3 is thousands.


3. If 1 is included, how many common factors do 30 and 48 have?
Ans.: Four (4) Sol.: The prime factorization of 30 is 2(3)(5). Hence, it has eight factors/divisors and these are 1, 2, 3, 5, 2(3), 2(5), 3(5), and 2(3)(5) or 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, and 30.
Of these eight factors/divisors of 30 only 1, 2, 3, and 6 are also factors of 48.
Hence, 30 and 48 have exactly four factors/divisors in common.


4. The sum of two numbers is 19 and their product is 84. What are they?
Ans.: 7 and 12 Sol.: Since the sum of the two numbers is positive and the product is also positive, each number must be positive.
So we can try number pairs like 1 and 18, 2 and 17, 3 and 16, 4 and 15, 5 and 14, 6 and 13, 7 and 12, 8 and 11, 9 and 10, among others.
One observes that the pairs (8,11) and (9,10) are too big, since 8(11)=88 and 9(10)=90 while the pair (6,13) is small as 6(13)=78. However 7(12)=84 and 7+12=19.


5. How many 18th are there in 5/6?
Ans.: Fifteen (15) Sol.: This answer is obtained by dividing 5/6 by 1/18 giving (5/6)/(1/18) = (5/6)(18) = (5/6)(6)(3) = (5)(3) = 15.
One can speedily verify that 15(1/18) = 5/6.


6. Change \( 6 \frac{7}{8} \) to improper fraction.
Ans.: 55/8 Sol.: \( a + \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a(c) + b}{c} \).
Here, \(a=6\), \(b=7\) and \(c=8\). So that \(6 \frac{7}{8} = \frac{(6)(8) + 7}{8} = \frac{48+7}{8} = \frac{55}{8} \)


7. If \(15/20 = N/100\), what is \(N\)?
Ans.: 75 Sol.: \( \frac{15}{20} = \frac{N}{100} \) hence \( \frac{3}{4} = \frac{N}{100} \). It follows that \( (3)(100) = 4(N) \) so that \( N = (3)(100)/4 = (3)(25) = 75 \).


8. Anna bought \(2 \frac{1}{4}\) dozens of eggs and used \(1 \frac{1}{3}\) dozens to make leche flan. How many eggs were left?
Ans.: 11 eggs Sol.: Since there are 12 eggs in a dozen, \( 2 \frac{1}{4} \) dozen eggs is equal to \( 12(2\frac{1}{4}) = 12(\frac{9}{4}) = 27 \;\) eggs. While \( 1 \frac{1}{3} \) dozen eggs is equal to \( 12(1 \frac{1}{3}) = 12(\frac{4}{3}) = 16\;\) eggs. The difference, \( 2 \frac{1}{4} \;\mbox{doz.} \;-\; 1 \frac{1}{3} \;\mbox{doz.}\; = 27-16 = 11\;\) eggs.


9. The ratio of girls to boys in a class is 3:5. If there are 40 pupils in the class, how many are boys?
Ans.: 25 are boys Sol.: \( \frac{40}{3+5} = \frac{40}{8} =5 \). So 3(5) are girls and 5(5) are boys.


10. There are 1200 Grade 4 pupils in Kalayaan Elementary School. If \( 45\% \) of them are girls, how many are boys?
Ans.: 660 are boys Sol.: \( 45\%\) of 1200 is \( 0.45(1200) = 540 \) so 540 are girls and 1200-540 = 660 are boys.


11. What is the supplement of the complement of a 42-degree angle?
Ans.: 132-degree angle or \(132^\circ\) Sol.: Complement of 42-degree angle is 90-42 = 48-degree angle.
The supplement of a 48-degree anlgle is 180-48 = 132-degree angle.


12. The radius of a circular park is 52 meters. If you jog around its edge, how many meters would you have jogged? Use \(\pi = 3.14\).
Ans.: 326.56 \(\;\)m Sol.: \( 2\pi r = 2\pi(52) = 104(\pi) = 104(3.14) = 326.56 \;\) m


13. What is the area of the circular park in 12?
Ans.: 8490.56 \(\;\)m\({}^2\) Sol.: \( \pi r^2 = \pi (52)^2 = 2704\pi = 8490.56 \;\) m\({}^2\)


14. Trees are planted 5 meters apart a long a straight line 135 meters long. How many trees are there?
Ans.: 28 trees Sol.: For a line 1(5)=5 m long, two trees; for a line 2(5)=10 m long, three trees; for a line 3(5)=15 long, 4 trees; ...; for a line 27(5)=135 m long 28 trees. So, in general, divide length of line of trees by the constant distance between then add 1, i.e., 135/5 + 1 = 27 + 1 = 28.


15. The total surface area of a cube is 216 square meters. How long is its edge?
Ans.: 6 meters Sol.: Let \(s\) be the length of an edge of the cube. Since a cube has six faces, each of the six faces is a square with edge of length \(s\), and the area of such a square is $ s^2 $, then
\( 6s^2 =216 \;\) m\({}^2\), so that \( s^2 = \frac{216}{6} = 36 \;\) m\({}^2\), and hence $ s = \sqrt{36} = 6\; $ m
